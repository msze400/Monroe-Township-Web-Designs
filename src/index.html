About Us

Monroe Township Web Designs

Hi, my name is Matt Sze! I’m the owner and developer behind Monroe Township Web Designs, and a proud Monroe Township native. I first got into programming during my undergrad years at Rutgers, where I worked as a MATLAB teaching assistant. Funny enough, I actually majored in Chemical Engineering and spent three years working in the oil and gas industry before realizing my real passion was in software development.

I quickly realized that career wasn’t for me and rediscovered my love for programming during the Covid pandemic, when I enrolled in Fullstack Academy’s Immersive Software Engineering bootcamp. After graduating, I joined Colgate-Palmolive as a developer, where I worked on their global eCommerce platform. From there, I transitioned to MIT Lincoln Laboratory in Boston, where I now work in the Artificial Intelligence and Algorithms division. I’m also currently pursuing my master’s in computer science at the University of Pennsylvania. My goal is to use the skills I’ve gained to give back — helping local businesses grow and succeed through modern, high-quality web solutions.

I want to help clients expand their business and help them get the most out of their online presence.

Our Working Process

We Take Care Of Everything

After a contract is signed we send a small questionnaire about your business and what you offer, then we expand on that content and write the entire site’s content for you, create the design, get approval, then we build. Generally takes 2-3 weeks from start to finish.

  • Step 1

    Complete Questionnaire

    We send you an email with less than 10 questions about your business, services, what sets you apart, etc and we do all the rest.

  • Step 2

    Design Approval

    We then do a video screen share showing you the first draft and make any changes you want to make sure you’re 100% satisfied.

  • Step 3

    Development

    Once approved, we can start coding the site out and setting everything up. We will send a demo link to view before we launch and go live.
